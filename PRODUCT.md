# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mobile-first casual gamers aged 16-45 who enjoy daily word puzzles. They play during commutes, breaks, or before bed. Many are multilingual. They want a quick mental challenge that feels rewarding without being stressful. Secondary audience: hardcore puzzle solvers who chase optimal scores and streaks.

## Product Purpose

LinkWords is a daily word association puzzle game. Players build chains of semantically connected words from a start word to an end word. Available in 20 languages, playable offline as a PWA, wrapping to native via Capacitor. Success looks like: a game people open every day out of habit, share results with friends, and never uninstall.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, satisfying, confident. The UI should feel like a premium instrument, not a toy. Every interaction should have weight and precision. Think: the tactile satisfaction of Block Blast's snap-into-place, the intellectual credibility of NYT Games, the addictive loop of Wordle's daily constraint.

## Anti-references

- **Candy Crush / Royal Match**: Too noisy, too many popups, pay-to-win mechanics, overwhelming particle effects, infantile color palette
- **Generic word games (Wordscapes, Word Connect)**: Dated UI, ad-heavy, clip-art aesthetics, no design ambition
- **Overly minimal web games**: Too sterile, no personality, no delight, just text on white
- **SaaS dashboard aesthetic**: Gradient cards, hero metrics, glassmorphism, corporate feel

## Design Principles

1. **Every tap should feel inevitable** — interactions are so intuitive they feel like the only possible design
2. **Density without clutter** — show everything the player needs, nothing they don't, at exactly the right moment
3. **Earn every animation** — motion serves comprehension or delight, never decoration
4. **World-class at any language** — the UI must feel native whether the player reads Arabic RTL or Vietnamese with diacritics
5. **Compete with the best, copy none** — match Block Blast / NYT Games quality through original design choices, not by mimicking their aesthetic

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Contrast ratio 4.5:1 minimum for all text. Full keyboard navigation. Screen reader support via aria-live announcer pattern. Reduce-motion preference respected (auto-detected from OS). Touch targets minimum 44x44px. RTL layout support for Arabic. Color-blind safe: never rely on color alone to convey game state.
